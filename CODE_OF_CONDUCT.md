# Code of Conduct

FTC Toolchain is a community for students, mentors, and developers. We want participation to be safe, respectful, and welcoming regardless of age, experience, identity, background, or team affiliation.

## Expected behavior

- Be patient, constructive, and specific.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Make room for beginners and explain unfamiliar terminology.
- Respect team privacy, competitive boundaries, and personal information.
- Accept maintainer decisions and review feedback professionally.
- Assume good intent while still taking harmful behavior seriously.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, discrimination, or sexualized language or imagery.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Pressuring students or other contributors to move conversations into private channels.
- Deliberately unsafe robotics advice, credential theft, malicious code, or attempts to damage another team's robot or systems.
- Sustained disruption, spam, or retaliation after moderation.

## Student safety

Many FTC participants are minors. Keep project collaboration in public repository channels whenever practical. Do not request personal contact details, school schedules, precise locations, robot Wi-Fi credentials, or other unnecessary identifying information. A parent, guardian, coach, or another trusted adult should be included when private coordination with a minor is genuinely necessary.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns confidentially through the repository's [private report form](https://github.com/Sanjit-K/ftc-toolchain/security/advisories/new) and begin the title with `[Conduct]`. Do not open a public issue that identifies a target of harassment or includes sensitive personal information.

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants whose behavior is inappropriate, threatening, harmful, or unsafe. Reports will be handled as privately as reasonably possible. Good-faith reports will not be punished.

This code applies in repository spaces and when someone is officially representing the project elsewhere.
